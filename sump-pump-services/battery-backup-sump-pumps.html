Frequently Asked Questions

A battery backup sump pump is an emergency system that activates when the primary sump pump fails or power is lost, ensuring water is diverted from the sump basin even during power outages.

The system includes a battery that automatically powers a secondary sump pump when the primary pump is unable to operate, keeping the sump basin from overflowing during power outages or primary pump failures.

Battery life depends on the capacity and usage but typically lasts between 4 to 10 hours of continuous pumping. Regular maintenance and testing can ensure optimal performance.

Regularly check and test the system, clean the sump basin, inspect battery for corrosion, ensure the pump is free of debris, and replace the battery every 3-5 years or as recommended by the manufacturer.

Depending on the system's capacity and battery strength, a battery backup sump pump can handle significant amounts of water, but it may not perform as strongly as the main sump pump during prolonged or extremely heavy rainstorms.
