Frequently Asked Questions

A slow-draining bathroom sink is often caused by a build-up of hair, soap, and other debris in the drain. You can clear it by removing any visible blockage, using a plunger, or applying a mix of baking soda and vinegar followed by hot water. If these methods fail, consider using a plumber's snake or contacting a professional plumber.

To prevent bathroom pipes from freezing, ensure they are well-insulated, especially in unheated areas. Allow a trickle of water to run from faucets during extremely cold weather, and keep the bathroom door open to allow warm air to circulate. Seal any cracks or openings in walls near the pipes to reduce cold air infiltration.

A continuously running toilet is often due to a faulty flapper, float level, or fill valve in the toilet tank. Begin by inspecting the flapper for a proper seal or replace it if it's worn out. Adjust the float arm or replace the fill valve if necessary to ensure the tank fills to the correct level. If you're uncomfortable with these adjustments, consult a plumber.

Low water pressure in a bathroom faucet might be caused by a clogged aerator, faulty valve, or blockages in the supply lines. Clean the aerator by unscrewing it and removing debris or mineral deposits. Check and adjust the shut-off valves under the sink, and inspect the pipes for any leaks or damage. If the issue persists, seek professional assistance.

To fix a leaky bathroom faucet, start by turning off the water supply. Disassemble the faucet to inspect washers, O-rings, and seals for wear or damage. Replace any worn components with matching parts, then reassemble the faucet. If leakage continues, consider replacing the faucet completely or hiring a plumber for intricate repairs.
