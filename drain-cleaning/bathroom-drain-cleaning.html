Frequently Asked Questions

To remove a clog from your bathroom drain, start by using a plunger to try to dislodge the blockage. If that doesn't work, you can use a mixture of baking soda and vinegar to break down the clog. Pour half a cup of baking soda followed by half a cup of vinegar down the drain, let it sit for about 30 minutes, then flush with hot water.

The best way to prevent hair clogs is by using a drain screen or hair catcher to stop hair from going down the drain. Regularly clean the screen or catcher and consider using a hair-catching brush or comb to remove loose hair before it reaches the drain.

While chemical drain cleaners can be effective for some clogs, they may damage pipes over time and pose environmental and safety risks. Use them sparingly and follow the instructions carefully. As an alternative, consider using natural methods like a baking soda and vinegar solution or a drain snake.

To prevent clogs, it's a good practice to clean bathroom drains once a month. This can be done by flushing them with hot water, using a biodegradable enzymatic cleaner, or applying a baking soda and vinegar solution.

Foul odors from bathroom drains are typically caused by bacteria and mold growth or a dry P-trap. To fix this, regularly clean the drain with a baking soda and vinegar solution, followed by hot water. Also, ensure the P-trap has water in it to block sewer gases.
