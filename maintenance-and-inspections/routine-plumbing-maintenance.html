Rising Demand for Regular Service

The growing population in Marshall has increased the strain on existing plumbing systems. Aging pipes, increased water usage, and infrastructure wear and tear result in the following issues:

  • Frequent Leaks and Drips : Unchecked leaks can lead to higher water bills and water damage.
  • Clogged Drains : From kitchen sinks to bathroom drains, blockages disrupt daily activities and may require professional intervention.
  • Low Water Pressure : Often caused by build-up in pipes, low pressure affects overall water flow.

Benefits of Routine Plumbing Maintenance

Frequently Asked Questions

It's recommended to check your plumbing for leaks every 3 to 6 months to catch any potential problems early and prevent costly repairs.

Signs of a clogged drain include slow draining water, unpleasant odors, gurgling sounds, and water backing up from the drain.

To prevent pipes from freezing, insulate exposed pipes, keep the thermostat set to the same temperature day and night, and let faucets drip slightly during extreme cold.

If you have low water pressure, first check for any visible leaks or sediment build-up in faucets. If the issue persists, consult a plumber as it may indicate a larger problem.

An unusually high water bill could indicate hidden leaks, faulty appliances, or recent changes in household usage. It's important to inspect your plumbing system and consider contacting a professional if the cause is not evident.
