Frequently Asked Questions

Backflow prevention is a method used to prevent contaminated water from flowing back into the clean water supply due to changes in pressure. It ensures that water flows in the intended direction within a plumbing system.

Backflow prevention is crucial for protecting public health by preventing contaminants or pollutants from entering potable water systems, potentially causing illness or health hazards.

Backflow is typically prevented using devices like backflow preventers, which include double-check valves, reduced pressure zone devices, and air gap separations that block unwanted reverse flow.

Backflow prevention devices should be tested annually by a certified professional to ensure they are functioning correctly and to comply with local regulations.

Backflow incidents can occur due to backpressure or back siphonage, often resulting from changes in water pressure such as during maintenance work, breaks in the water main, or high demand in the system.
